# New Product Launch & Go-To-Market

A launch is where strategy meets reality — and where most new products fail, usually for reasons that were predictable and preventable. Launches fail far more often from weak market understanding, poor positioning, and uncoordinated execution than from bad technology. This skill builds a coordinated, 360° launch plan and runs it through a pre-mortem against the documented failure modes.

## Trigger

User is launching a product, feature, service, or brand (new or into a new market), needs a go-to-market plan, launch timeline/checklist, launch-day plan, or asks why launches fail. Also trigger to operationalize an innovation or lifecycle "introduction" stage.

## Inputs

1. **What's launching** and how new it is (line extension → new-to-world)
2. **Target market/segment** and positioning (pull STP; for innovations pull `innovation-diffusion-strategy`)
3. **Value proposition and proof** (pull `value-proposition-design`)
4. **Timing / fixed dates** and geography (local / regional / global)
5. **Channels, sales team, budget**
6. **Readiness** — is product, pricing, supply, and support actually ready?

## Process

### 1. Confirm launch readiness (gate before spending)

Do not launch into a gap in the fundamentals. Verify:
- **Market fit** — validated need, sized opportunity, defined target (not "everyone"). Weak market understanding is the #1 killer.
- **Differentiated positioning** — a clear, relevant reason to choose this over alternatives and the status quo.
- **Whole product** — everything the target needs to get value (support, onboarding, integrations, docs), not just the core.
- **Pricing** — set from value, with the launch approach chosen (skim vs. penetration).
- **Operational readiness** — supply/capacity, channel stocked/trained, support ready, legal/compliance clear.
If any gate is red, fix it or scope the launch down before proceeding.

### 2. Set launch objectives and the beachhead

Define measurable launch goals (trial/signups, awareness, pipeline, share, activation) and pick the **beachhead**: the specific segment/geography to win first. For innovations, this is the chasm-crossing niche; for regional/global launches, decide what's standardized vs. localized (language, regulation, price, message). Concentrate force on the beachhead rather than diffusing across everyone.

### 3. Lock positioning and the launch narrative

Translate positioning into a launch story with an emotional and rational spine: the big idea, the customer insight it taps, the core message, and the proof. (Chevrolet Captiva's regional launch is a model: one big idea — "prepare yourself, people will look at you" — tied to a real customer insight, backed by design/comfort/performance proof, and adapted across markets.) Keep the message consistent across every touchpoint.

### 4. Design the 360° launch mix and timeline

Coordinate all levers on one timeline so they reinforce rather than collide. Typical components:
- **Pre-launch (tease/seed):** PR embargo/press, analyst/influencer briefings, teaser content, waitlist/pre-orders, channel and sales training, beta references.
- **Launch (peak):** announcement, advertising/media burst, launch event, product reveal, landing pages, demos/test drives, paid + owned + earned in concert, sales activation.
- **Post-launch (sustain):** always-on content, retargeting, reviews/testimonials, case studies, feedback loop, iteration.

Sequence with dependencies (e.g., landing page and pricing live before ads; sales trained before demand hits). Represent as a launch Gantt with owners.

### 5. Enable channels and sales

The best launch fails if the front line isn't ready. Provide: sales training and certification, demo assets, battlecards, pricing/quoting tools, channel incentives and stocking, and clear roles. For B2B, arm the team for the buying center (map to `b2b-buying-behavior`). Dealer/partner enthusiasm and readiness is often the difference between a flawless and a flat launch.

### 6. Set launch metrics

Define what "successful launch" means and instrument it before day one:
- **Leading:** awareness/reach, engagement, sign-ups/registrations, pre-orders, demo/test-drive volume, press coverage and sentiment, digital views/CTR.
- **Lagging:** trial→paid conversion, activation rate, sales/units, share in the beachhead, pipeline, CAC.
- Set targets and a review cadence; plan to reallocate spend fast based on early signals.

### 7. Run a pre-mortem against known failure modes

Before committing, imagine the launch failed and ask why. Check against the documented reasons new products fail:

| Failure mode | Guard |
|--------------|-------|
| Weak/absent market understanding | Revisit research, sizing, real need |
| Poor or undifferentiated positioning | Sharpen POD vs. alternatives + status quo |
| Wrong target / "everyone" | Commit to a beachhead |
| No whole product | Close the get-to-value gaps |
| Overpricing vs. perceived value, or value not communicated | Re-anchor to value; prove it |
| Uncoordinated execution / channel not ready | Tighten timeline, dependencies, enablement |
| Ignoring an incumbent or substitute response | War-game competitor reaction |
| Launching before operationally ready | Hold the gate |
| Right product, wrong market/timing | Re-check fit and moment |

Note the classic cautionary case: Coca-Cola Life (stevia, "green" positioning) launched into a crowded market and was withdrawn worldwide — a reminder that a well-executed launch still fails if the segment, differentiation, and reason-to-switch aren't compelling. Name the top 3 risks for *this* launch and the mitigation for each.

## Output

### Readiness Gate
Green/red on market fit, positioning, whole product, pricing, operations — with fixes for any red.

### Launch Strategy
Objectives (measurable), the beachhead, and the launch narrative/positioning.

### 360° Launch Plan
The pre-/launch/post mix on a timeline (Gantt), with dependencies and owners.

### Enablement
Channel and sales readiness plan.

### Metrics
Leading + lagging KPIs with targets and review cadence.

### Pre-Mortem
Top 3 failure risks for this launch and the mitigations.

## Follow-up

Ask: "Would you like me to:
- Draft the launch messaging, press release, and landing page?
- Build the detailed launch timeline/Gantt with owners?
- Model the adoption curve and set realistic launch targets?
- Create the sales-enablement kit (battlecards, demo script, FAQ)?"
