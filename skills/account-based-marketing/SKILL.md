# Account-Based Marketing (ABM)

When a few accounts represent most of the potential revenue (the B2B norm), it is more efficient to treat *each account as a market of one* than to spray a broad segment. ABM flips the funnel: pick the accounts first, understand their buying group deeply, and orchestrate personalized marketing-and-sales plays to win and grow them. This skill designs that program and keeps it grounded in real buying behavior and content that lands at the right journey stage.

## Trigger

User does B2B and needs ABM, key/named/strategic-account strategy, account selection and tiering, 1:1 / 1:few / 1:many program design, marketing-and-sales alignment, or B2B content mapped to the buyer journey. Especially when revenue concentrates in a defined set of high-value accounts.

## Inputs

1. **Offering and value proposition** (pull `value-proposition-design`)
2. **Ideal Customer Profile (ICP)** or the account list, if any
3. **Deal size and sales-cycle length** — justifies the ABM intensity tier
4. **Sales team structure** and how marketing and sales work together today
5. **Content and channels available**

## Process

### 1. Define the ICP and select accounts

Start from a sharp **Ideal Customer Profile**: the firmographic, technographic, and needs-based traits of accounts where you win, deliver value, and retain. Then build the target list by scoring candidate accounts on:
- **Fit** — match to ICP (industry, size, tech stack, use case)
- **Intent** — signals of active buying (research behavior, triggers, hiring, funding)
- **Potential value** — deal size + expansion/lifetime potential
- **Winnability** — relationships, incumbent lock-in, competitive position

Rank and cut to a manageable list. Reuse `market-research-sizing` to confirm the accounts represent real, reachable value.

### 2. Tier the accounts (choose the ABM model)

Match effort to value; not every account earns 1:1 treatment:

| Tier | Model | Personalization | Fits |
|------|-------|-----------------|------|
| Strategic | 1:1 ABM | Bespoke per account | Handful of highest-value/most-strategic accounts |
| Scale | 1:few ABM | Tailored to small clusters (by industry/use case) | Mid-tier; grouped by shared needs |
| Programmatic | 1:many ABM | Segment-personalized at scale via tech | Long tail of good-fit accounts |

State how many accounts sit in each tier and the resourcing implied.

### 3. Map the buying group per account

For each strategic account (and each cluster for 1:few), map the **buying center**: the roles (initiator, influencers, decider, buyer, users, gatekeepers), their goals and criteria, and the relationships between them. (Use `b2b-buying-behavior` for the full DMU/uncertainty analysis.) Identify champions, blockers, and the people sales currently can't reach — marketing's job is to influence those.

### 4. Build the account plan and plays

For each account/cluster, define:
- **Objective** — land, expand, defend, or reactivate
- **Insight** — the account's specific situation, triggers, and pains (researched, not generic)
- **Personalized value proposition** — the offer framed to this account's jobs and business case
- **Multi-touch plays** — coordinated actions across channels (executive outreach, tailored content/microsites, events/dinners, targeted ads, direct mail, sales sequences, referrals) aimed at specific buying-group members
- **Orchestration** — who (marketing + sales + SDR + exec sponsor) does what, in what order

### 5. Deliver content timed to the journey stage

B2B buyers now do most of their research before contacting sales, so digital content marketing is a strategic priority — but its effect depends on *timeliness and relevance to the buyer's current journey stage*, not on a fixed sequence. Map content to stages, and let the account's behavior (not a rigid drip) trigger the next piece:

| Journey stage | Buyer's question | Content that fits |
|---------------|-----------------|-------------------|
| Awareness | "Do I have a problem worth solving?" | Thought leadership, trend/industry insight, diagnostic tools |
| Consideration | "What approaches exist and who offers them?" | Comparison guides, webinars, technical explainers, ROI frameworks |
| Decision | "Why you, and can I trust it?" | Case studies, references, proof/demos, business case, security/compliance |
| Post-sale | "Am I getting value / should I expand?" | Onboarding, success content, expansion use cases, QBR material |

Deliver the *right* content at the *right* moment to the *right* role; irrelevant-but-well-produced content does not build engagement. Prioritize investment in the ability to target by account and stage.

### 6. Align marketing and sales

ABM fails without joint ownership. Establish: a shared target-account list, a shared definition of success (pipeline/revenue per account, not MQL volume), joint planning cadences, agreed hand-offs, and shared reporting. Marketing's KPI shifts from lead *quantity* to account *engagement and progression*.

### 7. Measure at the account level

Track account-centric metrics, not lead-volume vanity:
- **Engagement** — buying-group members engaged, depth/recency of engagement, web/content interaction per account
- **Progression** — accounts advancing through stages; pipeline created/influenced per account
- **Outcomes** — win rate, deal size, sales-cycle length, and post-sale expansion / net revenue retention in target accounts
- **Coverage** — % of buying group reached per account

## Output

### Target Accounts
ICP, the scored/tiered account list, and the resourcing per tier.

### Buying-Group Maps
Roles, champions, blockers, and reach gaps per strategic account/cluster.

### Account Plans & Plays
Objective, insight, personalized value prop, and the orchestrated multi-touch plays per account/cluster.

### Content-to-Journey Map
The stage × role × content grid and the trigger logic.

### Alignment & Measurement
The marketing-sales operating model and the account-level KPI set.
