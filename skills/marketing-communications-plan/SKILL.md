# Marketing Communications Plan

Communication is how positioning reaches the customer's mind and moves them to act. In B2B especially, it does jobs beyond awareness: it warms up prospects before a salesperson calls, reaches hidden influencers in the buying center, and stimulates derived demand downstream. This skill builds the communication program end-to-end and integrates the mix so every touchpoint reinforces one message.

## Trigger

User asks for a communications or promotion plan, messaging strategy, media/channel mix, advertising vs. sales-promotion balance, PR or content plan, lead-generation communication, or "how do we get the word out". Also trigger to operationalize the promotion lever of a marketing mix.

## Inputs

1. **Brand, target segment, and positioning** — the message must carry the position (pull STP)
2. **Communication objective** — awareness, education, lead-gen, preference, loyalty, reactivation
3. **B2B or B2C** — changes the mix weighting sharply
4. **Buying stage / customer journey** of the target
5. **Budget** (or budget-setting approach) and constraints
6. **Assets available** — case studies, data, references, content

## Process

### 1. Set communication objectives on the response path

Communication moves people along a hierarchy of effects; define the objective by *where the target is now* and where you need them next. The classic **AIDA** ladder: **Attention → Interest → Desire → Action** (extended: awareness → knowledge → liking → preference → conviction → purchase → loyalty). Early stages need reach and education; later stages need persuasion and a call to action. Set a measurable objective per stage.

### 2. Identify the target audience precisely

Beyond the buyer: define who to reach and their role. In B2B the audience is a **buying center**, not a person — communication must reach initiators, influencers, deciders, buyers, and users, several of whom the salesperson cannot access directly. Note that the specific communication goals in B2B often are:
- Create a favorable climate for personal selling (inform before the sales call)
- Reach influencers the salesperson can't (seniority gaps, gatekeepers)
- Reach *unknown* influencers in large/reorganizing accounts
- Reach channel intermediaries
- Stimulate derived demand (influence the end user even if not the direct buyer)
- Build a favorable corporate image on salient issues (e.g., sustainability)

### 3. Craft the message

Design the message to solve four problems: **what** to say (content/appeal), **how** to say it logically (structure), **how** to say it symbolically (format/creative), and **who** says it (credible source). Anchor content in the value proposition and the target's ranked pains/gains. Lead with attention because an unseen message can't work; then carry interest → desire → action. Keep the message consistent with the positioning and across every channel.

### 4. Select the communication mix

Choose the blend by objective, audience, stage, and B2B/B2C. Each tool has a distinct role:

| Tool | What it is | Strengths | Best for |
|------|-----------|-----------|----------|
| Advertising | Paid non-personal mass message | Reach, awareness, image | Broad awareness; leaders defend share here |
| Personal selling | Two-way interpersonal persuasion | Tailored, closes complex deals, builds relationships | High-value B2B, complex/technical sales |
| Public relations | Earned influence on attitudes/opinion | Credibility, low cost, image | Reputation, launches, issues, thought leadership |
| Sales promotion | Time-limited purchase incentive | Fast response, trial, volume | New entrants; trial and short-term lifts |
| Direct & digital | Personalized, data-driven outreach | Targeting, measurability, response | Lead-gen, nurture, e-commerce, ABM |

B2B tilts toward personal selling, trade fairs/events, technical press, references, and increasingly digital content; advertising is more supporting than lead. Note the pattern: **market leaders get more from advertising; new entrants get more from sales promotion.** Match the tool to the position.

### 5. Set the budget

Choose a budgeting method and pressure-test it:
- **Objective-and-task** (preferred) — cost the tasks needed to hit the objectives; ties spend to goals.
- Percentage-of-sales, competitive-parity, and affordable methods are common but weaker (they let last year's sales set this year's ambition).
Drivers that raise the needed budget: early lifecycle stage, low market share, large/growing market, strong competition, and high product substitutability.

### 6. Map to the customer journey / touchpoints (integrated planning)

Plan touchpoints across the journey and channels in one grid so the mix is coordinated, not siloed. For each touchpoint capture: segment, objective/KPI, content, timing, owner, budget, and external partners. This is the multi-channel plan — online (site, ads, social, email, webinars) and offline (events, trade press, sales, PR) working together.

### 7. Measure results

Split measurement into:
- **Pre-testing** — qualitative checks of concepts/messages before launch to pick the best and de-risk.
- **Post-testing** — qualitative + quantitative to track impact: awareness lift, engagement, leads, and the digital KPIs (impressions, reach, CTR, VTR, page views, unique visitors, stickiness, conversion). Tie each back to the stage objective it was meant to move.

## Output

### Objectives & Audience
Stage-based objectives on the response ladder; the target audience (buying center if B2B) with the specific B2B jobs where relevant.

### Message
The core message and the what/how/format/source decisions, tied to positioning and proof.

### Communication Mix
The chosen blend with the role of each tool and the leader/entrant logic.

### Budget
Method, total, and split — justified by the drivers.

### Integrated Touchpoint Plan
The multi-channel grid (touchpoint × segment × objective/KPI × content × timing × owner × budget).

### Measurement
Pre- and post-tests and the KPI set per objective.

## Follow-up

Ask: "Would you like me to:
- Draft the actual messaging, ads, or content for key touchpoints?
- Build the full campaign with a week-by-week content calendar?
- Design a B2B lead-gen / nurture sequence?
- Set the KPI dashboard to measure the plan?"
